Plutonium

Plutonium is the chemical element with the atomic number 94 and chemical symbol Pu. It is a highly radioactive metal, and is the metal used in most nuclear weapons. The chemical element plutonium has some different isotopes, sutch as 239Pu (or plutonium-239). It takes 24,000 years for a sample of plutonium-239 to decay, which is called its half-life. Plutonium-239 is commonly used to make nuclear weapons, and Pu-239 is a byproduct of nuclear reactor operations and nuclear bomb explosions.
Throughout its radioactive decay process, it generates a significant ammount of heat which makes it useful as a heat source for heating sensitive electrical components on satellites and powering said satellites.
